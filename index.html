Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who are we?

    Launched in 3020, Aibo is a company based in Abu Dhabi that started operating in 3012. Our company, first composed of a team of fewer than 20 individuals and 1 AI model, has now grown up to be a home for more than 10,000 employees, and 50 AI models. After global expansion, Aibo now has more than 1,000 bases worldwide. Aibo has been listed for several awards, including CNET’s Editors Choice Awards for January 3020, CES 3021 Innovation Awards, and entered the top ten list of Forbes's top Artificial Intelligence Companies of 3022.

  • How does Aibo work?

    Aibo is a program made for Neuralink that creates a virtual companion that is able to understand human emotion. Our cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence program analyzes the brain waves and behaviors of the user to create a virtual body, an Aibo. The new Aibo will be trained from scratch from the user's brain activity, ensuring complete compatibility for their new companion. Furthermore, the interaction occurs within the user neurons, ensuring complete control of it. Once the program is successfully installed, the user can communicate and interact with their Aibo as they would with a friend. Based on these interactions, Aibo will respond, give suggestions, help, or show emotions just as a good friend would. Aibo will develop personality traits that will improve the user’s living conditions and regularly adjust itself according to the user’s wishes. Be it an assistant, a companion, a friend, or even more, Aibo will help you feel understood and cared for.

  • Why will Aibo change my life?

    We are confident Aibo will change every single customer’s life. The simplicity of this program makes it accessible to a wide range of people. The sense of companionship generated by your private virtual character will transcend the reach of prior AI assistants, as it will respond directly to your emotions and brain activity. Aibo’s first aim was to fill the void of solitude and isolation in our users (aging populations). However, Aibo is currently open to the general public. There are no qualifications to join Aibo. Anyone needs a new friend!

  • Is Aibo safe?

    Our main priority in Aibo is our customer’s safety. We highly value keeping our customers' data and Aibos safe from cyber threats. At Aibo, we have built security measures and anti-hacking systems into every layer of Aibo’s programming. Firewalls, cryptography, and defensive engineering techniques are deployed to ensure that your behavioral data is safe with us.

    Aibo Privacy Statement:

    You have choices when it comes to the technology you use and the data you share. When we ask you to provide personal data, you can decline. Our product requires some personal data to provide you with a service. If you choose not to provide data required to provide you with a product or feature, you cannot use that product or feature. We share your personal data with your consent or to complete any transaction or provide any product you have requested or authorized. We also share data with Aibo-controlled affiliates and subsidiaries; with vendors working on our behalf; when required by law or to respond to legal process; to protect our customers; to protect lives; to maintain the security of our products; and to protect the rights and property of Aibo and its customers.

  • Can an Aibo be used to bring a loved one back from the dead?

    If the customer provides legal documentation of consent from the deceased for their image, voice and body to be used as an Aibo, our team will be more than happy to accommodate this request. Nevertheless, it is important to note that Aibo will not bring your loved one back from the dead. Our cutting edge Aibo will assume their image, and develop a personality based on your brain waves and predetermined training data. A legal waiver should also be signed by the user.

  • What happens to Aibo while we sleep?

    Your Aibo is hosted in our headquarters. The information is transmitted through the user’s Neuralink, allowing them to see and interact with their Aibo. When the user is sleeping, the Aibo will not be visible or active; one could say it is sleeping as well. However, Aibo records information about the user’s dreams and sleep-behavior; a pause for a data collection function is still in early development.
